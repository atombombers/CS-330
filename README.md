* How do I approach designing software?

When designing software, I focus on structure before getting into detail. In my 3D scene project, I began by building the overall layout with simple shapes to establish proportions and spatial relationships 
before refining the object textures and lighting. This helped me think in layers: first scene composition, then textures, then lighting. A key design skill I developed was understanding how different materials 
respond to light. Metal surfaces reflect sharp highlights while fabric absorbs light and requires softer ambient and diffuse balance. Working through this process strengthened my ability to break complex projects into 
stages, which I can apply to future software projects by prioritizing structure first and refinement second.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

* How do I approach developing programs?

When developing programs, I rely heavily on incremental development and controlled experimentation. Throughout the project, I tested small adjustments to lighting strength, material properties, scaling, 
and camera positioning to understand how each variable affected the final render. Instead of trying to perfect everything at once, I isolated aspects and refined them step by step. My development approach evolved 
from trial-and-error experimentation to more intentional adjustments, especially as I gained a clearer understanding. By the final milestone, I was writing and modifying code with greater confidence because I felt that I
better understood the full pipeline from object transformation to shading.

* How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?

Computational graphics and visualization expanded my understanding of the mathematical and technical foundations behind 3D rendering, including transformations, camera systems, and lighting models. Before this course, 
my experience with 3D modeling was primarily in Blender, where much of the complexity is abstracted away. Now I understand how matrices, shaders, and material properties work behind the scenes. These skills strengthen 
my educational pathway by deepening my problem-solving and system-level thinking, and they support my professional goals by improving my ability to debug complex systems, manage performance considerations, and design 
structured, scalable software. This project reinforced that I enjoy building interactive visual systems and gave me a strong technical foundation to expand into future personal or professional graphics work.
